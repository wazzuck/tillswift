Step 3

What switching
actually involves.

The rates are the easy part. Here's everything that needs to happen between "I want to switch" and "my new system is live and working" — and where most people get stuck trying to do it themselves.

1
Exit audit — check your current contract
Before anything else, your current provider needs to be checked for exit clauses, termination fees, and notice periods. Getting this wrong can wipe out months of saving before you've even started.
I review your current agreement, identify any exit costs, and factor them into the real switching calculation so there are no surprises.
2
Provider selection — right for your specific situation
The cheapest rate on paper isn't always the right choice. Outdoor market traders need reliable offline mode. Shop owners need solid POS software and inventory. Kiosk operators need compact, durable hardware and fast settlement. Some providers won't onboard certain business types at all.
I match the provider to your actual use case — not just the rate. That includes reliability, support response times, onboarding speed, and compatibility with any existing kit.
3
Hardware sourcing
Card readers vary widely in build quality, battery life, and weather resistance. The right hardware depends on your setup — a robust portable reader for a market stall, a countertop terminal for a shop, or a compact unit for a kiosk. Buying the wrong one is an expensive mistake. Refurbished options can save £50–£100 but need to come from the right sources.
I source the right hardware for your environment — with warranty, at the best price available. No guesswork on Amazon.
4
Merchant account setup & verification
Opening a merchant account requires ID verification, business registration details, and bank linking. Done correctly it takes 2–5 business days. Done incorrectly it can stall for weeks — or get rejected entirely.
I help you with the application, making sure the right documents are submitted in the right format so approvals go through first time — no back-and-forth with support.
5
Product catalogue & pricing setup
If you want itemised receipts, sales reporting by product, or low-stock alerts, your catalogue needs to be built properly from day one. This is tedious to do yourself and easy to get wrong.
I build your product catalogue, configure tax rates and categories, and set up reporting so your data is clean from the first transaction.
6
Full system test before go-live
Every component needs testing together — card reader, POS app, receipt flow, payout routing to bank. A test transaction doesn't tell you much. A full end-to-end run does.
I run a complete pre-launch test and sign off that every part of the system works before you take your first real payment.
7
Go-live day support
Launch day problems happen. A card reader that won't connect, an app update that breaks something, a payout that doesn't arrive — all of these are solvable, but not while you're serving customers.
I'm available on launch day to deal with anything that comes up, so you can focus on selling.
8
Staff handover & training
A new payment system is only useful if everyone using it is confident with it. Most providers offer a help centre. That's not the same as a 20-minute walkthrough with someone who set it up.
I walk through the system with you and any staff — refunds, daily close, reports, what to do if the reader loses connection. Hands on, not a YouTube link.
9
Ongoing first-line support
After setup, small things come up — a software update changes the interface, a reader needs replacing, a payout is delayed. Most traders don't have the time or inclination to deal with a provider's support line.
I remain your first call. I either fix it directly or navigate the provider's support on your behalf — saving you the hold time and the headache.
